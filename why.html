"The Great Filter Theory"

There is a theory, proposed by economist Robin Hanson in an essay from 1996 entitled "The Great Filter - Are We Almost Past It?", that tackles the failure of humanity to detect any life beyond Earth. This "Great Filter" is an event, or circumstance, in the evolution of intelligent life that creates a very low probability of a particular "civilization" successfully moving past this chokepoint.

The question for humanity is whether this chokepoint lies behind us somewhere in our evolutionary past, or remains in our future. Of course, there could also be multiple chokepoints, and successfully traversing one does not guarantee future success.

It is my belief that the 21st Century might be such a filter for us.

The scope of the Maker Village Project does not encompass existential threats - those events that would result in the extinction of our species (i.e. a massive asteroid strike, an intense high-energy gamma ray pulse from a nearby dying star, cascading self-replicating nano-tech, a hyper-efficient bioweapon, true Artificial Intelligence, etc.)

Rather, this project asks the question of how can we most efficiently create a safety net, an insurance policy, a seed for technological and cultural recovery from an event that radically changes modern civilization and throws us back into a new Dark Ages. Our modern, global society has become extremely complex, and with that complexity comes the greater risk of a cascading failure - the concept that one failure leads to the failure of connected components until the entire system experiences catastrophic failure.
